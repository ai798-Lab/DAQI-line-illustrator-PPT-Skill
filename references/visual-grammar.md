# Visual Grammar

## Composition

- Use a portrait canvas, commonly 2:3 or 3:4.
- Keep one large central or lower-half illustration.
- Allow one thin border or one large rounded frame, but do not make it a UI card.
- Use asymmetry: figure may be cropped by the edge, prop may cross the body, accents may be off-center.
- Keep 35%-60% of the canvas visually quiet.

## Line Work

- Use thin black strokes, usually 2-5 px on a 1000 px canvas.
- Draw with open contour paths, not closed cartoon outlines.
- Lines may overlap and pass through objects.
- Use loops, bends, and hand-like repeated curves.
- Do not fill the body with color; the figure remains mostly line.

## Accent Shapes

- Red: baton, pin, dot, bar, lever, signal, route marker.
- Yellow: crown, map, spotlight, jagged burst, folded sheet.
- Indigo: tiny dot only.
- Accent shapes should be flat and bold, with no outline or very little detail.

## Density Budget

- One dominant figure or hand.
- One core prop.
- One or two large accent shapes.
- Three to six tiny dots, ticks, or dashed-route marks.
- No more than one scribble cluster.

## Prohibitions

- No typography by default.
- No logo, badge, icon set, dashboard, wireframe, chart, or workflow diagram.
- No realistic face details, clothing folds, furniture, room, landscape, or crowd.
- No busy background pattern.
